Five evaluation axes

Five Axes

Every audited tool receives an independent score on each axis (0.0–5.0), combined into a single overall grade from S to D.

01 — Practicality

How well the tool performs the actual job in real workflows: task completion, UX, integration, and consistency.

02 — Cost Performance

Value relative to price: pricing transparency, free-tier utility, lock-in risk, and ROI analysis.

03 — Japanese Readiness

Quality of Japanese UI, business honorifics, font handling, documentation, and localized support.

04 — Safety

Security posture, compliance with the Japanese Personal Information Protection Act, audit logs, and incident response.

05 — Uniqueness

Differentiating technology, ecosystem, roadmap transparency, and dependency on third-party APIs.

Why independence matters

Independence

Reproducible methodology

Every score is tied to a specific methodology version. Changes are tracked publicly in our score changelog.

Quarterly re-audits

Tools are re-audited on a 90-day cycle, plus on major version bumps and security incidents — keeping scores fresh.
